3.Origin of Yoga, Meditation, and Tapas

Yoga has its roots deep in ancient India, born from the intense search of sages and rishis who sought truth beyond the material world. They turned inward, into silence and solitude, practicing meditation beneath trees, in caves, and on mountains — not for comfort, but for liberation.

Through deep meditation, they discovered the union between the body, mind, and the eternal soul. This union is the meaning of "Yoga" — from the Sanskrit word "Yuj", meaning to join or to unite with the Divine.

Their practice was not easy. It was filled with tapas, which means heat, discipline, and inner effort. Tapas is the fire of dedication — the will to sit still, face discomfort, and overcome the ego. Through tapas, the body is purified, the mind is sharpened, and the spirit is awakened.

The sage in the image symbolizes this sacred journey — calm, focused, and connected to higher truth. Surrounded by divine presence and seated under a sacred tree, he reminds us that yoga is not just exercise, but a lifelong path of self-discovery and inner strength.

7.1 what is Vrikshasana

Vrikshasana – The Tree Pose

Vrikshasana, also known as the Tree Pose, is a graceful standing yoga posture that symbolizes balance, stability, and inner strength. The word "Vriksha" means "tree" in Sanskrit, and just like a tree stands tall and rooted to the ground, this pose encourages us to stay strong and centered even in the face of challenges.

In this asana, one leg is firmly grounded while the other is lifted and placed on the inner thigh or calf of the standing leg. The hands are joined in Namaste above the head, resembling the branches of a tree reaching for the sky. Practicing Vrikshasana helps improve body balance, concentration, and posture. It strengthens the legs, tones the muscles, and brings a deep sense of calm and focus.

Beyond its physical benefits, Vrikshasana also teaches us an important life lesson — to stay grounded like the roots of a tree while growing upward toward our goals. It connects the practitioner with nature and encourages a peaceful and mindful state of being. Simple yet powerful, Vrikshasana is a beautiful expression of harmony between the body and mind.

12.Importance of Practicing Yoga

Yoga is more than just physical exercise — it is a way of life that brings balance to the body, mind, and soul. Practicing yoga regularly helps us stay healthy, calm, and focused, no matter how busy or stressful life may be.

Physically, yoga improves strength, flexibility, and posture. It keeps our body active, boosts energy levels, and helps prevent many health problems. Mentally, it reduces stress, improves concentration, and brings peace of mind. Through breathing techniques and meditation, yoga teaches us how to stay calm and handle emotions better.

Spiritually, yoga connects us with our inner self. It encourages mindfulness, self-awareness, and a deep sense of harmony with nature and others around us.

In today's fast-paced world, yoga is a powerful tool to stay grounded and healthy. That is why millions of people around the world, young and old, practice yoga daily. On June 21, International Yoga Day, we celebrate this ancient gift that continues to guide us toward a life of wellness, peace, and happiness.

13.The Importance of International Yoga Day

International Yoga Day, celebrated on June 21st, is a reminder of the ancient wisdom of India that has now reached every corner of the world. Yoga is more than just a series of physical exercises—it is a path to harmony, bringing together the body, mind, and spirit.

In today’s fast-paced world, where stress, anxiety, and poor health have become common, yoga offers a simple yet powerful solution. It teaches us how to breathe deeply, live mindfully, and stay balanced in both joy and sorrow. The practice of yoga promotes inner peace, improves physical health, and strengthens our mental focus.

International Yoga Day encourages us all to make yoga a part of our daily lives, helping us become healthier, happier, and more centered human beings.
